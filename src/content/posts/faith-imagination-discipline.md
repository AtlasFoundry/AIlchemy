## The shiny phase doesn't last

There's a particular kind of energy that shows up at the beginning of learning something new. Everything feels possible. The tools are exciting. Progress feels fast because the baseline is zero.

And then it stops feeling that way.

This is the moment that matters. Not the beginning — the middle. The part where you've learned enough to know what you don't know, and the gap feels enormous.

## What actually gets you through

I've been through this cycle enough times now to know what works for me. It's not motivation. Motivation is weather. It comes and goes.

What works is something more structural.

**Faith** — not the religious kind, but the operational kind. A working assumption that if you keep showing up, something will shift. You don't need to believe this deeply. You just need to act as if it's true long enough for the evidence to catch up.

**Imagination** — the ability to hold a picture of what you're building in your head before it exists. This is what keeps the work from feeling pointless on the days when nothing quite works. You're not lost; you're just mid-build.

**Self-discipline** — the boring one. The one nobody talks about because it's not glamorous. Showing up when you don't feel like it. Finishing things. Not letting perfect be the enemy of done.

## The honest version

I don't always have all three. Some weeks I have faith but my imagination has gone quiet and I'm running on discipline alone. Other weeks the imagination is firing but the discipline is thin.

The trick, I think, is knowing which one is low and going looking for it deliberately. More often than not, you just needed to name the problem.

## What this has to do with AI

Everything, actually. Learning to use these tools well is a long game. The people who are going to get the most out of it aren't the ones who had the best first session. They're the ones who kept going past the shiny phase and built something real.

That's what AIlchemy is about.
